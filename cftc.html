Code for the Caribbean

SlashRoots' Code for the Caribbean Fellowship Programme pairs teams of technologists with forward-thinking government agencies in the Caribbean to develop digital tools that help improve the design and delivery of public sectors.

Around the world, innovative governments and citizens are rethinking how government can operate in the 21st century using the power of the Internet. This new "civic technology" is enabling new approaches to delivering public services and catalyzing new models of collaboration between the public service and the communities it serves.

Launched in 2013, the Code for the Caribbean Fellowship programme, aims at catalyzing these developments in the region. In the inaugural class, the Fellowship programme was hosted by the Rural Agricultural Development Authority (RADA) and focused on the endemic problem of praedial larceny. Praedial Larceny is the theft of the agricultural produce and livestock and robs the farming community in Jamaica of more than US$50 million annually.

Implementing for Real Impact // Making Real Progress

The Code For the Caribbean Fellowship team has helped make real progress towards addressing one facet of the complex issue of praedial larceny. HarvestAPI, a platform that manages and shares agricultural data across government agencies and with the public, is helping break down information silos between different agencies charged with serving the agricultural community, such as the RADA, the Jamaica Agricultural Society and the Jamaica constabulary force. The platform then enabled the fellows to develop Clip, a SMS-based information service geared towards giving police office better access to information in the field to support decision making.

The SlashRoots team has continued working with the RADA and the recently launch Praedial Larceny Unit to institutionalize both HarvestAPI and Clip and implement a pilot programme.
