Developing the Caribbean (DevCa)

Developing The Caribbean is the region’s marquee platform for discussing open data, digital governance and innovation for addressing development issues. A roll-up-your-sleeves solutions focused event, that brings together innovators from the public sector, civil society and development organizations with civic-minded technologists, researchers, and entrepreneurs.

caption

Launched in 2012, DevCa has grown to become one of the largest innovation and social impact conferences in the region. Conceived initially as a regional conference and code sprint in which teams of designers, developers and domain experts work together to build solutions to social problems using open data. It has grown to include workshops on mapping, programming and data journalism, reflecting the growing interest in the use of data across multiple disciplines. Events have been organized across the region in Jamaica, Trinidad & Tobago, The Dominican Republic, Cuba, Guyana and many other islands.

In 2015, DevCa brought together more than 340 attendees participating in 8 events across 4 countries. The event expanded from two-day event to a month long series of activities, creating a “big tent” in which distinct communities can find common ground to share experiences, lessons learned from ongoing work, and collaborate to unlock the potential greater adoption of open data and digital government can have on the Caribbean’s development.

Our Role

As a lead organizer for DevCa we help define the strategic direction of the event and work with each local organizing partner to make their events a success.

Why is this important to us?

We believe the Caribbean is strongest when we work together. DevCa is both a celebration of Caribbean openness and a platform to further Champion the cause of a more open and collaborative region.
